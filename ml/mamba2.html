Where we left off· Mamba-1

A selective state-space model

$$ h_t = \st{A_t}\,h_{t-1} + \st{B_t}\,x_t \qquad y_t = \st{C_t^{\top}}\,h_t $$

The parameters $A_t, B_t, C_t$ depend on the current token — the model chooses what to keep.

  • Linear-time inference, constant-size state
  • Content-aware, selective memory
  • Strong long-context modeling
But one question was left open — and it was about hardware, not accuracy.
The big question

Why do Transformers train so well?

Because modern GPUs are built for one thing — large dense matrix multiplication.

A V        →  big matmul
linear     →  big matmul

Everything is dense matmul. Tensor cores stay busy.

What Mamba-2 wants

Keep the wins, gain the hardware

Can a model hold on to everything Mamba-1 earned and compute like a Transformer?

The surprising result

One object, two faces

A specific class of state-space models can be rewritten, exactly, as a form of structured attention.

They were never competing paradigms — they are two ways of computing the same structured sequence transformation.

What is a duality?

Same object, different coordinates

A duality is when two systems that look unrelated turn out to produce exactly the same result.

Representation A
$$ a + b\,i $$
Representation B
$$ r\,e^{i\theta} $$

One complex number. Two descriptions. Mamba-2 applies this exact idea to sequence models.

Attention, stripped down· 1 / 6

Forget the softmax

Let $\at{A}=\at{QK^{\top}}$. Then attention is just a matrix product:

$$ Y = \at{A}V \qquad y_i=\sum_{j\le i}\at{A_{ij}}\,v_j $$

Each output is a weighted sum of earlier values. Causality makes the matrix lower-triangular.

causal attention matrix · $A\in\mathbb{R}^{T\times T}$
First insight· 2 / 6

The matrix is the point — not the softmax

How people think of it
Attention  =  QKᵀ  +  softmax
What it actually is
Attention  =  a structured matrix

Once you see attention as “multiply by a matrix,” the only interesting question left is: what structure does that matrix have?

Now the SSM· 3 / 6

Unroll the recurrence

Take the scalar selective SSM and just expand it term by term:

$$ h_t=\st{a_t}h_{t-1}+\st{b_t}x_t,\qquad y_t=\st{c_t}h_t $$
Expanding the state
$h_1=\st{b_1}x_1$
$h_2=\st{a_2 b_1}x_1+\st{b_2}x_2$
$h_3=\st{a_3a_2b_1}x_1+\st{a_3b_2}x_2+\st{b_3}x_3$
a pattern is already forming ↑
The hidden matrix· 4 / 6

A row of a matrix product appears

Read out $y_3=\st{c_3}h_3$ and group by input:

$$ y_3=\underbrace{\st{c_3a_3a_2b_1}}_{M_{31}}x_1+\underbrace{\st{c_3a_3b_2}}_{M_{32}}x_2+\underbrace{\st{c_3b_3}}_{M_{33}}x_3 $$

That is literally $y_3=M_{31}x_1+M_{32}x_2+M_{33}x_3$ — one row of a matrix multiplication.

The whole map· 5 / 6

The entire SSM is one matrix

$$ Y=\st{M}X $$
$$ \st{M_{ij}}=\st{c_i}\Big(\textstyle\prod_{k=j+1}^{i}\st{a_k}\Big)\st{b_j}\quad(i\ge j) $$

Lower-triangular, exactly like causal attention. Entries are products of decays — so they fade down each column.

structured SSM matrix · entries $\st{c_i(\prod a_k)b_j}$
The SSD observation· 6 / 6

Two models, the same shape

Attention
$$ Y=\at{A}V $$
State space model
$$ Y=\st{M}X $$

Both are matrix multiplications. The whole story collapses to one question — what structure does the matrix have?

Why dense attention hurts

A dense matrix is enormous

A full attention matrix stores every pairwise interaction:

$$ \at{A}\in\mathbb{R}^{T\times T}\;\Rightarrow\; T^2 \text{ entries} $$
At T = 100,000 tokens
$$ T^2 = \at{10^{10}} $$

ten billion numbers — impossible to store explicitly.

Why the SSM is cheap

The matrix is never built

Every entry is generated by the same three sequences:

$$ \st{M_{ij}}=\st{c_i}\Big(\textstyle\prod_{k=j+1}^{i}\st{a_k}\Big)\st{b_j} $$

Store only $\st{a_t},\st{b_t},\st{c_t}$ per step — not the whole grid.

Storage
$$ \st{O(T)} \;\;\text{vs}\;\; \at{O(T^2)} $$

Same operator. A vanishing fraction of the memory.

The bridge structure

Semiseparable matrices

$M$ looks dense — but every block below the diagonal has low rank.

  • Dense to look at, tiny to store
  • Off-diagonal blocks factor as $UV^{\top}$
  • This family is the mathematical bridge between SSMs and attention
amber blocks are low-rank — represented by a handful of vectors
What low rank buys

A little information, a huge matrix

Stored densely
$$ 1000\times1000 = \at{10^{6}} $$

a million numbers

As $UV^{\top}$, rank 16
$$ U,V\in\mathbb{R}^{1000\times16}=\de{32000} $$

~30× compression

Semiseparable structure is what lets a $10^{10}$-entry operator cost $O(T)$.

Mamba-2’s one change
Mamba-1 · diagonal state
$$ A_t=\begin{bmatrix}a_{t1}&0&\cdots\\0&a_{t2}&\cdots\\ &&\ddots\end{bmatrix} $$

a different decay per state dimension

Make $A$ a single scalar

Mamba-2
$$ A_t=\st{a_t}\,I $$

One shared decay across all state dimensions. It looks weaker — and then something magical happens.

Suddenly, attention

The factorization that gives it away

A shared decay makes $M$ factor cleanly:

SSM (Mamba-2)
$$ \st{M}=\de{L}\circ\big(\st{C}\st{B^{\top}}\big) $$
Attention
$$ \at{A}=\at{Q}\at{K^{\top}} $$
L = decay factors  ·  C = output projections  ·  B = input projections  ·  ∘ = elementwise mask

A low-rank interaction plus a structural modifier — the same shape as $QK^{\top}$.

One model, two computations

The duality, in your hands

Recurrent · for inference

A familiar analogy

Like the DFT and the FFT

As a matrix
$$ y=Fx $$

direct, dense

As an algorithm
$$ \mathrm{FFT}(x) $$

recursive, fast

Same operator, two computations. The attention form and the state-space form are the DFT and FFT of one sequence transform.

The SSD algorithm

Chunk the sequence

  • Diagonal blocks — within a chunk, compute the quadratic (attention) way: dense matmuls on tensor cores
  • Off-diagonal — between chunks, pass one summary state: the linear (scan) way
Quadratic inside chunks, linear across them — matmul-efficient and scalable at once.
diagonal = matmul · off-diagonal = state passing
