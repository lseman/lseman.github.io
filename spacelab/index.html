From concept to final product, our team covers every Technology Readiness Level, developing unique custom-built systems for a broad range of space applications.

Research

Research, innovation and teaching are the main principles of SpaceLab. Technological advances allow on-board systems to become more and more autonomous.

Technological advances allow on-board systems to become more and more autonomous, not only for the navigation and attitude control activities of a spacecraft, but also in the pre-processing of data to be transmitted to ground stations. In the big data era, on-board pre-processing is a de facto need in modern satellite platforms.

The multidisciplinary nature of the field is an inspiration for unfolding new technologies, which could be useful also in other application fields such as autonomous robots guidance, self-driving cars, among others.

Ongoing missions & projects

FloripaSat-2

1U/2U CubeSat Constellation · 100% development · 1 yr mission
Awaiting Launch

The FloripaSat-2 mission is composed of FloripaSat-2A and FloripaSat-2B, two nanosatellites developed under the FloripaSat-2 platform to validate a Brazilian CubeSat architecture created by SpaceLab. Each with its own power, processing, and communication subsystems operating in commercial frequency bands.

Both satellites will be launched from the Alcântara Space Center (CLA) in the Spaceward 2025 mission, using a commercial launch vehicle called HANBIT-TLV by INNOSPACE, to place them into a low Earth orbit of 300 km.

GOLDS-UFSC

2U CubeSat Satellite · 100% development · 1 yr mission
Integration

An educational mission where students are involved with the design and operation of a CubeSat system in a space environment. Students learn about radio communications, digital communications, radio electronics, and antennas.

An amateur radio mission will be implemented where the satellite functions as a digital store-and-forward repeater. The mission will have global coverage and be accessible to any radio amateur.

Payload-XL / GOMX-5

Rad-Hard FPGA · 85% development · 1 yr mission
In Dev

Payload designed at SpaceLab, in partnership with ESA/ESTEC, for the GOMX-5 mission. GOMX-5 is a 12U satellite from GomSpace, Denmark. Payload-XL used the BRAVE NG-Large FPGA from NanoXplore, France.

Catarina Constellation

2 × 2U CubeSat Satellites · 90% development · 1 yr mission
In Dev

FloripaSat's bus to be used in the Catarina Constellation satellites under development at SpaceLab. Officially adopted by the Brazilian Space Agency (AEB) in the National Space Activities Program.

Microgravity-II

Sub-Orbital Launch Mission · 70% development · 1 yr mission
In Dev

Research and development of scientific payloads to be used in a sub-orbital flight campaign.

Competition

In order to train the next generation of undergraduate students for future opportunities, the lab supports initiatives for taking part in contests and events in the aerospace sector.

FloripaSat Team

Designing and building prototype CubeSats for competitions

Founded in the first half of 2018, the FloripaSat team emerged to participate in the 1st CubeDesign competition. The team consists of undergraduate and graduate students from the Federal University of Santa Catarina.

Electrical Engineering Department, UFSC

Apex Rocketry

Building rockets for competitions

Founded in the second half of 2018, Apex Rocketry emerged from the initiative of a student group to develop and build mini-rockets. Subdivided into three areas — Electronics, Aerostructures and Propulsion — the team consists of undergraduate students from UFSC.

Mechanical Engineering Department, UFSC
Back to home // 04 — Ground Segment

Groundstation

Ground station, our operation centre. The Ground Segment consists of all ground-based elements for communication with our satellites.

Community

Join the community

SpaceLab receives essential support from the amateur radio community. The amateur radio community is essential to help receiving telemetry from small satellites transmissions.

Acknowledgment

ARAF Partnership

Without our partner ARAF — Associação dos Radioamadores de Florianópolis — missions like FloripaSat-1 would not be possible. Their support in tests and during mission operations is fundamental for success.

Opportunities

Get involved

Radio knowledge and expertise is fundamental for successful missions in SpaceLab. If you have experience with radio communication, participate in a group of amateur radio or want to learn more, get in touch.

Events

Amateur radio events

SpaceLab participates and supports amateur radio events in Florianópolis. Check our news page for the next events.

Our ground station

The Ground Segment

It includes our Ground Station and the operations center. The Ground Station is the radio station for communication with the satellites. It can send/receive communications in the VHF and UHF bands. It consists of antennas, a server and screens for visualization.

Further improvements are planned to automate the system, provide external access and keep uninterruptedly monitoring CubeSat missions.

Software

Ground Station Software

Developed an application that performs low-level tasks using a Software Defined Radio (SDR) device, through a graphical interface. Designed for the core system of FloripaSat missions and validated after the launch of FloripaSat-1.

The application is available in the laboratory GitHub repository and is open source.

13 Jun 2026

CENIPA and INNOSPACE complete technical investigation report on Operation Spaceward

Last Friday, June 12, INNOSPACE announced that CENIPA, linked to the Brazilian Air Force, completed the final investigation report on Operation Spaceward, carried out in December 2025 at the Alcântara Space Center. The release of the document formally closes the investigation process and enables the continuation of subsequent launch activities. SpaceLab participated with FloripaSat-2A and FloripaSat-2B satellites.

03 Jun 2026

SpaceLab/UFSC opens opportunity to fly an experiment aboard a new satellite

SpaceLab is opening an opportunity for companies, laboratories, startups, and educational institutions interested in flying an experiment aboard the FloripaSat-RO satellite. The initiative aims to broaden access to the space environment for new partners, strengthening connections among different sectors of society.

SpaceLab is preparing for new satellite launches following the loss of two payloads during a previous launch attempt. The next mission is scheduled for the end of this year and will send nanosatellites into orbit in partnership with SpaceX.
